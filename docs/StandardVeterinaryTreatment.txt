#### 1. System Context & Legal Protocols

* 
**SVTG Status:** Advocacy guidelines developed by DAHD, Government of India, with FAO and USAID. They provide economic, evidence-based frameworks to enhance care uniformity, ensure food safety by cutting residues in animal-source foods (ASFs), and mitigate global Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR).

#### 2. Clinical Pathologies: Etiology, Signs & Interventions

##### Ruminant Systemic & Metabolic Conditions

* 
**Atresia Ani & Atresia Recti:** Pathognomonic visual presentation (imperforate anal opening) removes the need for differential diagnosis. *Treatment:* Surgical correction via a 2 to 3 cm circular incision at the anal site. For atresia recti, free the blind end of the rectum from adhesions and suture the mucosa to the skin using non-absorbable material.


* 
**Goitre:** Iodine deficiency manifesting as thyroid enlargement in newborns. *Treatment:* Administer potassium iodide orally to affected lambs. *Prevention:* Provide potassium iodate-enriched salt blocks (@ 200 mg/kg salt). Pregnant ewes should receive 280 mg potassium iodide or 370 mg potassium iodate individually during months 4 and 5 of gestation.


* 
**Hypovitaminosis A:** Manifests in young stock as brain and spinal cord compression; adults present with night blindness, corneal keratinization, pityriasis, hoof defects, weight loss, and infertility. *Pathology:* Classified into primary (absolute lack of carotene/vitamin A in diet) and secondary (interference with digestion, absorption, or metabolism at tissue level).


* 
**Septic Arthritis:** Inflammatory condition in calves caused by bacteria (e.g., *E. coli*, *Salmonella*, *Mycoplasma*) or joint trauma. *Clinical Signs:* Depression, fever, local pain, anorexia, joint swelling, stiffness, and joint crepitus.


* **Traumatic Reticulopericarditis:** Evaluated using advanced cardiac biomarkers including cardiac Troponin I, lactate dehydrogenase-I, and creatinine kinase. Advanced fluid buildup management uses pericardiocentesis via an 18-gauge spinal needle placed at the left fifth intercostal space.
* 
**Thermal Burn Injuries:** Categorized by depth and severity. *First-Degree:* Erythema, oedema, and desquamation of superficial skin layers. *Second-Degree:* Erythema, oedema at the epidermal-dermal junction, epidermal necrosis, blister, and eschar formation. *Third-Degree:* White to black wounds, loss of epidermal/dermal components, painless eschars, and hypovolemic burn shock.


* 
**General Poisoning / Toxicosis:** Manifests via skin contact, inhalation, or ingestion of household chemicals (antifreeze, rodenticides, pesticides) or toxic plants. Inhaled toxins include carbon monoxide and fumes from bleach or smoke.



##### Infectious Diseases of Large & Small Ruminants

* **Anthrax:** Per-acute or acute septicaemic disease caused by *Bacillus anthracis* (gram-positive, spore-forming, highly fatal). Characterized by sudden mortality and incomplete rigor mortis. Splenic tissue and peripheral blood smears reveal encapsulated bacilli. Strict carcass disposal rules apply.
* 
**Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD):** Highly contagious viral disease characterized by pyrexia and vesicular eruptions on the tongue, lips, teats, and interdigital space. *Etiology:* Foot and Mouth Disease Virus (FMDV), a non-enveloped, single-stranded positive-sense RNA virus belonging to the genus *Aphthovirus*, family *Picornaviridae*. Serves 7 distinct serotypes: O, A, C, Asia1, SAT1, SAT2, SAT3.


* 
**Bovine Viral Diarrhoea (BVD):** Pestivirus infection presenting with mucosal erosions, severe enteritis, and leukopenia. Diagnosed via histopathological evaluation and viral antigen detection. *Differential Diagnosis:* Must be clinically differentiated from FMD and malignant catarrhal fever.


* **Enterotoxaemia (Pulpy Kidney):** Acute toxemic disease caused by *Clostridium perfringens* (primarily epsilon toxin), inducing systemic vascular endothelial damage and brain lesions. Targets fast-growing lambs on high-grain diets.
* 
**Caprine Arthritis Encephalitis (CAE):** Caused by Caprine Arthritis Encephalitis Virus (CAEV). Young animals show CNS signs: depression, head tilt, circling, opisthotonos, torticollis, and hind limb pedalling. Adults show chronic interstitial pneumonia, dyspnea, and hard udder (firm, enlarged mammary glands with agalactia). Lungs appear hard and greyish-pink with tiny white foci. Gross lesions include reddish-pink swellings in cervical/thoracic spinal segments.


* 
**Caseous Lymphadenitis:** Chronic infectious disease caused by *Corynebacterium pseudotuberculosis*, manifesting as abscesses in peripheral lymph nodes (parotid, submandibular, prescapular). Abscesses start firm and painless, eventually rupturing to discharge thick, cheesy green-white pus.



##### Standalone Species Specific Conditions

* **Equine Infectious Anaemia (Swamp Fever):** Persistent equine viral disease caused by Equine Infectious Anaemia Virus (EIAV), a *Lentivirus* in the *Retroviridae* family. Transmitted mechanically via the mouthparts of biting insects (tabanids/stable flies) in swampy ecosystems.
* 
**Botulism (Equine/Multispecies):** Paralytic disease caused by neurotoxins of *Clostridium botulinum*. Caused by eating feed or drinking water contaminated with decaying carcasses or spoiled forage. Causes progressive flaccid paralysis.


* 
**Gastrointestinal Pathologies (Equine):** Acute enteritis/colitis presentations. *Diagnosis:* Radiography establishes intestinal motility profiles and obstructions; endoscopy allows direct visualization of mucosal surfaces and biopsy collection. *Treatment:* Immediate rehydration via intravenous or oral fluids to fix severe electrolyte shifts.



#### 3. Diagnostic Sample Collection Framework

* **Brucellosis Screening:** Relies on Rose Bengal Plate Test (RBPT) and Serum Agglutination Test (SAT) for field screening, confirmed via ELISA.
* 
**T3/T4 Diagnostic Profiling:** Deficiencies like goitre require whole blood collection in plain vials for serum separation to run T3, T4, and metabolic hormone assays.


* **Oxyuris equi (Pinworm) Detection:** Tape-press method using clear adhesive tape applied directly to the perianal skin to trap eggs for immediate microscopic confirmation.
* **Viral Pathogen Isolations:** Requires active lesions, vesicle fluid, unruptured scabs, lymph node aspirates, or whole blood collected in heparin/EDTA.
* 
**Mastitis Sampling:** Clean, aseptically drawn mid-stream milk samples collected before infusing intramammary antibiotics. Samples must be placed immediately on ice and transported to the laboratory.


* **Postmortem Tissue Processing:** Requires fresh blocks of spleen, lung, liver, kidney, and mesenteric lymph nodes preserved in 50% buffered glycerine or transported on dry ice.

#### 4. Focused Veterinary Pharmacology Metrics

* **Norfloxacin:** Dosed at 10 mg/kg Body Weight (BW) administered via intramuscular (IM) route; shows fast transfer into milk channels for clinical mastitis management.
* **Levamisole:** Administered at 2.5 mg/kg BW; used as an immunomodulator adjunct to enhance primary antimicrobial or vaccine responses.
* 
**Mange Therapeutics:** For *Sarcoptes* and *Psoroptes* infestations, administer systemic Ivermectin at a targeted dose of 0.2 mg/kg BW.


* **Iron Dextran:** Haematinic therapy for iron-deficiency anemias; dosed at 2.0 mL via deep IM injection.
* 
**Equine Antibiotic Administration Guidelines:** Avoid co-administration with drugs inducing hypokalemia (like thiazide or loop diuretics). Glycyrrhizin acid (liquorice extract) can modulate therapeutic outcomes.


* 
*Penicillin G:* 22,000 IU/kg BW administered intravenously (IV) every 6 hours.


* 
*Procaine Penicillin G:* Administered exclusively via deep IM route.
