### HAH RAG Master Knowledge Base: High-Density Dataset

#### 1. Pathological Definitions & Temporal Classifications

* 
**Disease States:** Classified by onset, progression, and physiological source.


* 
*Acute:* Sudden onset, high severity, short duration.


* 
*Chronic:* Slow progression, lower immediate intensity, long duration.


* 
*Infectious:* Caused by biological pathogens (viruses, bacteria, fungi, parasites) entering and multiplying in host tissues.


* 
*Contagious:* An infectious disease subtype transmitted directly or indirectly from host to host.


* 
*Zoonotic / Public Health:* Diseases transmissible between vertebrate animals and humans under natural conditions (e.g., *Leptospirosis*, *Rabies*, *Brucellosis*, *Listeriosis*).





#### 2. Clinical Pathologies: Etiology, Systems & Indications

##### Respiratory & Systemic Bacterial Pathologies

* 
**Hemorrhagic Septicemia (HS):** Acute bacterial infection caused by specific pasteurella strains. It produces systemic endotoxins causing high vascular permeability, rapid depression, severe respiratory distress, and high mortality rates. Frequently triggered by stress factors such as long-distance transport.


* **Strangles:** Highly contagious equine upper respiratory tract infection presenting with purulent nasal discharge and acute swelling/abscessation of submandibular and retropharyngeal lymph nodes.
* 
**Erysipelas:** Swine pathology presenting in an acute form as high fever and septicemia, or a chronic form characterized by distinctive, pathognomonic diamond-shaped raised reddish skin lesions across the body.



##### Neurological & Musculoskeletal Conditions

* 
**Rabies:** Neurotropic viral infection causing fatal encephalomyelitis in all warm-blooded species. Manifests as extreme behavior changes, including aggressive/furious forms, hydrophobia, paralysis, hyper-salivation, and vocal changes.


* **Tetanus (Lockjaw):** Toxemic condition driven by *Clostridium tetani* spores in deep anaerobic wounds. Neurotoxins block inhibitory neurotransmitters, causing continuous tonic muscular spasms, third-eyelid prolapse, and rigid "sawhorse" posture.
* 
**Infectious Arthritis (Septic Joint):** Bacterial colonization of joint cavities, frequently stemming from navel-ill (*omphalophlebitis*) in neonates, causing acute lameness, heat, severe local pain, swelling, and joint crepitus.



##### Parasitic Entropies (External & Internal)

* 
**Coccidiosis:** Protozoan intestinal disease targeting mucosal cell walls, leading to blood-stained diarrhea, dehydration, and emaciation. *Host specificity constraint:* Species-specific protozoan lines govern expression, with sheep and goats being the primary species exceptions capable of cross-sharing identical strains.


* **Oxyuris equi (Equine Pinworm):** Parasitic infestation of the large intestine. Gravid female worms migrate to the perianal skin to deposit eggs within a sticky gelatinous fluid, inducing intense perianal pruritus and tail-rubbing behaviors.
* **Liver Flukes (*Fasciola spp.*):** Trematode parasites that damage liver parenchyma during migration before maturing within bile ducts, inducing chronic anemia, hypoproteinemia, and structural ascites ("bottle jaw").

#### 3. Diagnostic Modalities & Postmortem Processing

* 
**Physical Diagnostics:** Relies on structural analysis:


* 
*Ocular examination:* Evaluates mucous membranes for jaundice (hepatic breakdown), pallor (severe anemia), or petechiae (systemic toxemias).


* *Perianal Tape-Press Method:* Uses clear adhesive tape applied firmly to the perianal region to catch and isolate *Oxyuris equi* eggs for direct microscopy.


* 
**Fecal Analysis Routines:** Three core processing methods for finding parasite eggs:


* 
*Direct Smear:* Rapid identification of high-density protozoal trophozoites or heavy egg shedding.


* 
*Sedimentation:* Preferred for dense trematode eggs (flukes) that drop out of solution.


* 
*Floatation:* Uses high-specific-gravity solutions to force lightweight nematode and cestode eggs to the surface meniscus.




* **Necropsy & Carcass Rules:** Postmortem tissue extraction must target fresh blocks of the spleen, lungs, liver, and deep mesenteric lymph nodes. *Biosecurity rule:* In sudden mortalities where anthrax is a differential, do not open the carcass to avoid environmental spore formation. Normal deep burials require carcasses to sit deeper than 1 meter to block scavenging vectors from digging up contaminated tissues.



#### 4. Pharmacological Parameters & Dosing Metrics

* 
**Cloxacillin:** Narrow-spectrum, penicillinase-resistant penicillin compound indicated exclusively for sensitive Gram-positive bacterial infections, particularly useful in mastitis protocols.


* 
**Xylazine:** Alpha-2 adrenergic agonist acting as a powerful sedative, muscle relaxant, and analgesic. *Equine warning:* Do not use as a standalone restraint when working near the hindquarters of horses, as it does not completely suppress defensive kicking reflex arcs.


* 
**Ivermectin:** Macrocyclic lactone broad-spectrum antiparasitic compound highly effective against most internal nematodes and external arthropod parasites. *Breed contraindication:* Do not exceed basic therapeutic doses in Collie dog breeds or related lines due to multi-drug resistance (MDR1) blood-brain barrier gene deletions.


* 
**Phenylbutazone:** Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) configured to treat musculoskeletal pain and pyrexia. *Contraindication:* Completely ineffective at managing visceral pain or spasms associated with equine colic.


* 
**Oral Antibiotic Caution:** Avoid administering broad-spectrum oral antibiotics to adult ruminants/herbivores, as it disrupts or destroys the symbiotic microflora of the rumen and cecum, causing fatal dysbiosis.
